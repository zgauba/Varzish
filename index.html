Program Strategy

Why This Works For You

GLP-1 microdosing suppresses appetite and aids fat oxidation — but it also risks muscle loss if protein intake and resistance training are insufficient. This program counteracts that directly: progressive calisthenics preserves and builds lean tissue while the caloric deficit does its job.

Daily Time Commitment

  • Strength/Full-body days: 35–45 min
  • Mobility days: 20–30 min
  • Active recovery: 20 min walk or light stretch
  • Rest day: Full rest or gentle walk

Phase Progression (12 Weeks)

  • Weeks 1–4 (Foundation): Build habit, activate muscles, improve flexibility baseline
  • Weeks 5–8 (Build): Add reps/sets, introduce harder progressions
  • Weeks 9–12 (Peak): Full intensity, visible definition, deep squat achieved

GLP-1 Notes

On injection day (and day after): lighter activity is fine — skip strength if nauseous. Prioritize hitting protein targets especially on low-appetite days. Nausea is temporary; muscle loss from skipping protein is not.

Hydration

Target 3–3.5L per day. GLP-1 increases dehydration risk. Start each morning with 500ml water before food. Hydration directly affects flexibility and joint health.

Desk Warrior Fix

Slouch. Daily.

The Problem

What Computer Work Does

  • Tight hip flexors — from constant sitting, pulls lumbar into anterior tilt
  • Weak glutes — inhibited from sitting, causes lower back pain
  • Forward head posture — every inch forward = +10 lbs load on cervical spine
  • Rounded shoulders — pec tightness, weak mid-back (rhomboids/lower traps)
  • Thoracic kyphosis — upper back rounds, reduces breathing capacity

Ergonomic Setup

Quick Desk Wins

  • Monitor height: Top of screen at eye level
  • Chair height: Feet flat on floor, knees at 90°
  • Lumbar support: Small pillow or rolled towel at lower back
  • Screen distance: Arm's length away
  • Set a timer: Get up every 60 min. Walk 2 min. Non-negotiable.
